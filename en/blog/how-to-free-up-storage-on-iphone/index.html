How to Free Up Storage on iPhone: 8 Effective Methods

Learn how to free up storage on your iPhone by reviewing photos, videos, unused apps, downloads, and other space-consuming content.

Author: The AP Design

Published: 2026-07-16 Updated: 2026-07-16

7 min read

To free up storage on your iPhone, first check which content is using the most space. Then review large videos, unnecessary photos, duplicate media, unused apps, message attachments, downloads, and other files you no longer need. If you use iCloud Photos, you can also turn on Optimize iPhone Storage.

A full iPhone does more than stop you from taking new photos. It can also make everyday tasks such as installing apps, updating iOS, and downloading files more difficult. Instead of deleting random items, it is healthier to start with the areas that use the most storage and move through them carefully.

Check iPhone Storage First

Before you start cleaning up, check which apps and content types are using your storage:

  1. Open the Settings app.
  2. Go to General.
  3. Tap iPhone Storage.
  4. Review how much space your apps and content types are using.

On this screen, you can see how much space Photos, Messages, installed apps, and other categories are using. Your iPhone may also show storage recommendations based on your current usage.

Apple’s guide to checking iPhone storage

If Photos is taking up the most space, start with your gallery. If apps are the main issue, removing or offloading apps you rarely use can give faster results.

1. Delete Unnecessary Photos and Videos

Photo libraries usually grow quietly over time. Multiple shots of the same moment, forgotten screenshots, images saved from social media, screen recordings, and long videos can take up a significant amount of storage.

When cleaning up your gallery, you can use this order:

  • Review unnecessary screenshots.
  • Delete blurry or accidental photos.
  • Choose between similar shots of the same moment.
  • Check screen recordings you no longer need.
  • Prioritize large and long videos.
  • Review media saved from social media apps.

Choosing between thousands of photos can feel tiring. Instead of trying to finish your entire gallery in one session, reviewing a small section each day is usually more sustainable.

3. Check the Recently Deleted Album

Media you delete in the Photos app does not disappear completely right away. Deleted photos and videos stay in the Recently Deleted album for 30 days. During that period, they can be recovered or permanently deleted sooner.

To check the Recently Deleted album:

  1. Open the Photos app.
  2. Go to Collections.
  3. Scroll down and find Recently Deleted.
  4. Unlock the album with Face ID or your passcode.
  5. Permanently delete items you no longer need.

If you need storage immediately, deleting photos from your library may not be enough; you may also need to empty Recently Deleted.

You may not be able to recover photos and videos after permanently deleting them. Check carefully before starting the final delete action.

Apple’s guide to Recently Deleted and permanent deletion

4. Merge Duplicate Photos and Videos

Multiple copies of the same photo can cause unnecessary storage use over time. You can use the built-in Duplicates feature on iPhone to merge detected duplicate photos and videos:

  1. Open the Photos app.
  2. Go to Collections.
  3. Under Utilities, tap Duplicates.
  4. Use Merge next to the duplicate group you want to combine.

If your library does not contain detected duplicates, this section may not appear.

This feature is helpful for identical copies. For similar but not identical photos, you may still need to review your library manually and decide which shots to keep.

Apple’s iPhone guide to merging duplicate photos and videos

5. Offload or Delete Unused Apps

Apps you have not opened in a long time can use storage through both the app itself and the documents and data they create.

You can choose between two approaches:

  • Delete App: Removes the app and its related data.
  • Offload App: Removes the app while keeping its documents and data.

To let iPhone automatically offload unused apps:

  1. Open Settings.
  2. Go to Apps.
  3. Tap App Store.
  4. Turn on Offload Unused Apps.

This option can reduce the storage used by apps you rarely open while helping preserve their data.

Apple’s iPhone guide to offloading unused apps

6. Review Large Messages Attachments

High-resolution photos, videos, and other files sent through messages can use a lot of storage over time.

Open Settings > General > iPhone Storage, then review the Messages section to inspect large attachments and content categories.

Before deleting attachments:

  • Save important documents.
  • Check files from family or work conversations.
  • Do not bulk delete items only because they are large.

Older conversations that include long videos can often free up noticeable space quickly when reviewed carefully.

7. Optimize Storage with iCloud Photos

If you use iCloud Photos, you can keep original photos and videos in iCloud while storing smaller device-optimized versions on your iPhone.

To do this:

  1. Open Settings.
  2. Tap your name at the top.
  3. Go to iCloud > Photos.
  4. Turn on Sync this iPhone.
  5. Select Optimize iPhone Storage.

To use this feature, you may need enough iCloud storage and an internet connection for syncing.

If iCloud Photos syncing is on, deleting a photo from your iPhone may also delete it from other devices synced with the same Apple Account. Do not treat iCloud Photos as a separate backup system by itself.

8. Remove Downloads and Offline Content

Offline content from apps such as Netflix, Spotify, YouTube, podcasts, and map apps can also fill up your iPhone storage.

Check these areas:

  • Downloaded TV shows and movies
  • Downloaded music
  • Podcast episodes
  • Offline maps
  • Files downloaded through Safari
  • Large documents in the Files app

Because most of this content can be downloaded again when needed, it can be a safe place to start.

Important Precautions Before Cleaning iPhone Storage

To avoid losing data while cleaning up:

  • Check that important content is backed up.
  • Remember that iCloud Photos storage and iPhone device storage are different concepts.
  • Preview items one last time before deleting many of them.
  • Review Recently Deleted carefully before emptying it.
  • Do not give full photo access to apps you do not trust.
  • Read the privacy explanations of cleanup apps.
  • Clean up regularly instead of trying to finish everything at once.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does iPhone storage fill up so quickly?

High-resolution photos and videos, app data, message attachments, downloaded files, and offline media can all cause iPhone storage to fill up quickly.

Why did deleting photos not free up space?

Deleted photos and videos may still be stored in the Recently Deleted album. If you need space right away, check that album and permanently delete items you no longer need.

Does buying iCloud storage increase my iPhone storage capacity?

No. iCloud storage and your iPhone’s physical storage are different. However, iCloud Photos and Optimize iPhone Storage can help reduce the space used on your device.

Is offloading an app the same as deleting it?

No. Offloading removes the app while keeping its documents and data. Deleting an app can remove the app and its related data.

Is it safe to use a gallery cleanup app?

It depends on how the app works and how it handles privacy. Check whether photos are uploaded to servers, which permissions are requested, and whether the app asks for confirmation before permanent deletion.

Conclusion

Reviewing large videos, unnecessary photos, duplicate content, unused apps, and old downloads on a regular schedule can help prevent your storage from filling up again too quickly.
